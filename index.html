Enabling customization through Machine Learning

Ingka Group, the largest IKEA franchisee, recently acquired Geomagical labs who are developing a solution to enable customers to design their favorite spaces in a mixed reality experience (real + virtual). Using just a smartphone, customers are able to quickly capture their space and virtually experience IKEA products in their room before purchasing them.

Finding lines is critical to understanding the shape and contents of indoor spaces: wall seams, table edges, and other key lines provide important information.

IKEA combines neural networks and computer vision techniques to locate and label key lines in the scene in both 2D and 3D.

Indoor panoramas normally require a special tripod mount or wide-angle lens. This app uses neural networks, photogrammetry, and world-class stitching algorithms to combine photos into a seamless, beautiful panorama.

Step 1
How it works:
Scene capture

The first step is to capture the space you wish to redesign. Users with a smartphone will be able to use the IKEA app to take an ultra wide, "magazine quality" photo of their room.

A parallax tolerant algorithm enables them to blend the captures without any stitches into an ultra wide photo.

  • check Line detection
  • check Parallax tolerant algorithm

Combines photogrammetry and multiple neural networks to recreate the full detailed 3D structure of the room and its contents, achieving world-class accuracy. Photogrammetry uses multiple photos of the room, taken from different angles, to triangulate the distance of walls and other objects.

Neural networks and computer vision techniques enable the software to understand what's in the room, classifying every visible object and creating a semantic model for every single detection within it.

Step 2
How it works:
3D models

Next, the app will create a detailed and scale-accurate 3D model of the captured room, and all of its contents, so that users can realistically redecorate.

  • check Depth estimation
  • check Segmentation

IKEA models are rendered into the room with realistic-looking shadows, illumination, scale, and occlusions.

The software combines classic computer vision algorithms and deep learning techniques to accurately detect light sources in the room. This enables the technology to render reflections, shadows, and a realistic appearance of the virtual furniture.

Step 3
How it works:
Designing the space

The last step is to ensure the 3D room looks and feels like your room, with high quality and accuracy.

  • check Realistic rendering and light source estimation

Once the room panorama is created, IKEA customers will be able to access their room anywhere, delivered through the Google Cloud. They can design their spaces on the go, and literally walk about with their dream home in their pocket.

It's all about leveraging technology to create more seamless customer experiences

IKEA leverages Google Cloud's AI solutions to personalize its digital experiences like never before.

Recommendations AI

Recommendations AI delivers product recommendations, such as ‘other items you may like’, ‘recommended for you’ and ‘frequently bought together’, enabling users to design their dream homes faster and easier with IKEA. This can be done on a global scale with machine learning models that are constantly being updated to improve the results.

Vision API Product Search

Using Vision API Product Search, IKEA simplifies the return process throughout its different platforms. Google Cloud Product Search applies machine learning to compare and find the most similar item of all IKEA products. Employees at the return department at IKEA can quickly identify an item by taking a photo of them.
